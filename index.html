About the County

Hidden in the Appalachian Foothills

Jackson County sits between Charleston and Parkersburg along the Ohio River — close enough to reach from half a dozen states, far enough to feel like another world. A place people return to every July 4th, that draws hundreds of thousands of visitors a year across its marquee events, and that has been quietly worth visiting for over 150 years.

Its two towns have distinct characters. Ripley, the county seat, is the kind of American small town that gets voted Most Community Pride and actually deserves it. Ravenswood, on the Ohio River, was named after a hero from a Walter Scott novel and is building something new on the banks of one of America's great rivers.

Two Towns, Countless Reasons

Ripley and Ravenswood sit 11 miles apart and cover everything from Appalachian craft spirits to Ohio River sunsets.

Conference & Event Venue

Four miles from downtown sits West Virginia's most-visited venue — 450 acres with four lakes, lodging for hundreds, and year-round events and workshops.

Craft Distillery

Ripley is home to one of WV's most distinctive craft spirits producers — traditional moonshine and unique WV flavors made cornstalk to bottle.

Water Trail

A 20+ mile kayak journey from Ripley to Cottageville along Mill Creek — one of West Virginia's most accessible and scenic water trails.

Artisan Market & Historic Theatre

Over 100 local artisans, live performances, concerts, and film at a Main Street landmark operating since 1936.

City Park & Recreation

Olympic-size pool with water slide, skateboard park, lit tennis courts, picnic shelters, and a gazebo. Ripley's community hub — free to visit.

Local Dining & Shopping

From WV-style hot dogs to hand-cut steaks, local boutiques to artisan goods — Ripley's independent businesses are worth exploring. See our Dining and Shopping sections.

Trail System

The Kevin Clegg Trail System at Eastwood Recreation Area — nine trails totaling 13 miles across 120 acres. Hiking, mountain biking, and trail running for all skill levels. Actively expanding.

Ohio Riverfront

Washington's Riverfront Park on the Ohio River — fishing, boat launch, bird watching, walking trails, and a performance stage hosting major acts each August.

Local History & Museum

Native American roots through the 20th century, including a remarkable early-1900s photography exhibit documenting Ravenswood in extraordinary detail.

Campground & Marina

Full RV hookups, boat slips, and direct Ohio River access — within 45 miles of three major markets.

Ohio River Festival

Major performers, fireworks by Premier Pyrotechnics, a crazy boat race, and a full carnival at Washington's Riverfront Park each August.

Downtown Revival

Ravenswood's OnTRAC Main Street program is bringing new energy to Washington Street. New businesses, events, and the annual Harvest in the Wood festival.

Events Worth the Drive

Jackson County runs on community. These are the annual events that have been drawing people for generations.

July 2–5, 2026  ·  Annual

Independence Day Celebration

Ripley, WV  ·  Downtown & Courthouse Lawn

America's Largest Small-Town Independence Day. Six days — a 200-unit parade, carnival rides, Firecracker 2-Miler, watermelon contest, and free concerts. 2026 performers: Darryl Worley, Bucky Covington, Johnny Staats Project. Population swells from 3,000 to 20,000.

Free Entry Official site

July 3–5, 2026  ·  Annual

Mountain State Art & Craft Fair

Cedar Lakes Conference Center, Ripley

One of West Virginia's premier craft events during July 4th weekend. Regional artisans, folk art, handmade goods, and workshops. Founded as part of WV's 1963 Centennial — seventh decade running.

Cedar Lakes Details

July 2026  ·  Annual

Jackson County Junior Fair

Jackson County Fairgrounds, Cottageville, WV

Youth projects, livestock competitions, carnival rides, local food, and community traditions. A cornerstone of Jackson County's farming heritage and one of the year's most family-friendly events.

Family Event

August 2026  ·  Annual

Ohio River Festival

Washington's Riverfront Park, Ravenswood

Three days at the Ohio River with major performers, fireworks by Premier Pyrotechnics, a crazy boat race, pet pageant, and carnival rides.

Riverfront Park

Fall 2026  ·  Annual

Harvest in the Wood

Downtown Ravenswood, WV

Ravenswood's beloved autumn festival. Local vendors, artisans, food, and fall color along the historic Washington Street corridor.

Downtown Ravenswood

November 2026  ·  Annual

Veterans Day Parade

Ripley, WV  ·  Jackson County Courthouse

Hundreds of photo posters of veterans and current service members lead a uniquely moving parade to a courthouse ceremony.

Free & Open

Plan Your Visit

A Perfect Jackson County Weekend

Two unhurried days from Columbus, Pittsburgh, or Charleston — about a 2.5-hour drive. Here's how to spend them.

Day One

Ripley & Cedar Lakes

  • MorningArrive via I-77 Exit 138. Coffee and a walk around downtown Ripley, the kind of Main Street that earns its community-pride reputation.
  • MiddayDrive four miles to Cedar Lakes — 450 acres, four lakes, disc golf, trails, and the Staats Mill Covered Bridge. Pack a picnic or eat on the grounds.
  • AfternoonTour Appalachian Distillery for cornstalk-to-bottle moonshine and WV flavors — paw paw, blackberry, peach.
  • EveningDinner in Ripley — hand-cut steaks, WV hot dogs, or Mexican — then check in to your lodging.

Day Two

Ravenswood & the Ohio River

  • MorningDrive 11 miles north to Ravenswood. Hike or bike the Kevin Clegg Trail System at Eastwood Recreation Area — 13 miles, all skill levels.
  • MiddayLunch downtown, then Washington's Riverfront Park on the Ohio River — boat launch, fishing, and wide-open river views.
  • AfternoonPaddlers: put in on Mill Creek for part of the 20+ mile Ripley-to-Cottageville water trail. Or browse the local history museum.
  • EveningCatch an Ohio River sunset before the drive home — or stay for whatever festival is on the calendar.

Best Time to Visit

There's a reason to come in every season — not just the Fourth of July.

Spring

Quiet trails, rising creeks for paddling, and green hills before the summer crowds. The mildest weather for hiking.

Summer

Peak season. The legendary July 2–5 Independence Day celebration, the Mountain State Art & Craft Fair, and the August Ohio River Festival. Book lodging months ahead.

Fall

Appalachian foliage, the Harvest in the Wood festival in Ravenswood, and the best photography light of the year along the river.

Winter

The quiet off-season — distillery tastings, cozy small-town dining, and the moving Veterans Day Parade carrying into the holidays.
